FAQ

Is white water rafting dangerous?

White water rafting can be thrilling and challenging, but it does come with inherent risks. However, with proper safety measures, trained guides, and appropriate equipment, the risks can be minimized. It's important to choose a reputable rafting outfitter and follow their safety guidelines.

What is white water rafting?

White water rafting is a recreational outdoor activity that involves navigating down rivers or streams in an inflatable raft. Participants work together as a team to paddle through rapids and obstacles, guided by an experienced rafting guide.

What equipment do I need for white water rafting?

The necessary equipment typically includes a helmet, personal flotation device (PFD) or life jacket, and a paddle. The outfitter will provide the raft and any additional gear required, such as wetsuits or splash jackets, depending on the water temperature and weather conditions.
